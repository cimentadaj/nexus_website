The headline the two-event structure makes possible
Cities and national governments agree on building the intelligent city — and diverge sharply on who it is for. Alignment runs at 71% on the intelligence economy but falls to 38% on belonging and inclusion.
For the first time, one platform reads the city conversation on 31 January and the national conversation across 1–3 February against the same four tracks. The gap is not a problem to hide — it is the agenda for the room.

Two Ways to Run This

January pilot, or a standing capability

Everything in this platform can be delivered as a one-off for the 2027 edition. But the real value compounds: the inaugural edition sets a baseline, and continuous monitoring turns a single snapshot into a measurable impact record across editions.

Option A · Edition pilot

The January Snapshot

Delivered for the inaugural edition. Everything in tabs 1–4, live for the event.
  • Full analysis of city & national documents across the four tracks
  • City ↔ national alignment study, ready for the cross-event roundtable
  • Track coverage, gaps and emerging-vocabulary read
  • Insight pack for the marketing, programme and comms teams
  • The 2027 baseline, fixed and citable
Option B · Standing observatory

Continuous Coverage

The pilot, plus everything a baseline unlocks once it runs every year.
  • Re-read after each edition — measure what actually moved
  • Impact attribution: which themes travelled from stage to city documents
  • Diffusion lag, tracked live as cities adopt over time
  • Year-on-year alignment trend between the two summits
  • A defensible, data-backed impact story for every future edition
